# Child Safety and Protection Policy

**Last updated: June 28, 2026**

Tether ("we," "our," or "us") has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). This policy applies to all users of the Tether app and covers all content shared on our platform.

---

## Zero Tolerance

We do not permit any content, behavior, or activity on Tether that sexually exploits or abuses minors in any way. This includes but is not limited to:

- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind
- Grooming, solicitation, or sexual communication directed at minors
- Sharing, distributing, or linking to CSAE content
- Any attempt to use Tether to exploit or abuse a minor

Violations of this policy will result in immediate and permanent account termination.

---

## Reporting

If you encounter any content or behavior on Tether that you believe involves the sexual exploitation or abuse of a minor, please report it immediately:

**Email:** [bryant.b.allred@gmail.com](mailto:bryant.b.allred@gmail.com)  
**Subject line:** CSAE Report

You may also report directly to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) at [www.missingkids.org/gethelpnow/cybertipline](https://www.missingkids.org/gethelpnow/cybertipline) or contact your local law enforcement.

---

## How We Respond

Upon receiving a report of potential CSAE content or behavior, we will:

1. Remove the reported content immediately pending review
2. Suspend the reported account pending investigation
3. Permanently terminate the account if a violation is confirmed
4. Report confirmed CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) as required by law (18 U.S.C. § 2258A)
5. Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations

---

## Prevention

To reduce the risk of misuse, Tether is designed so that:

- All connections are mutual and must be accepted by both parties
- There are no public profiles or public content feeds
- Content is only visible to users who are directly connected
